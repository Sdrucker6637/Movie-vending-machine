How This Works

16 slots, one movie each. Tap an empty slot to add a movie, or tap a full one to see where to watch it and swap it for something else. Everything's saved on this device automatically.

Search by title, or tap 🎲 Surprise me for a random pick instead of typing one in.

Popular — well-known movies, spread across different decades rather than just what's trending now.

Indie/obscure — well-reviewed but lower-profile titles you probably haven't heard of.

Curated — a short survey (genre, vibe, decade, runtime, director, actor, plot/themes) - answer as few or as many as you like.

Similar to… — search a movie you like and get something in the same spirit.

Picks at random from whatever's actually loaded in your 16 slots right now - good for "just tell me what to watch tonight."

TMDB (required) — powers search, posters, synopses, trailers, and where-to-watch info. Free. Get a key at themoviedb.org/settings/api, then paste it in via the ⚙ Settings button.

Gemini (optional) — makes the Vibe and Plot/themes fields in Curated understand abstract descriptions ("trippy," "cheesy," "coming of age") that TMDB's own search can't. Free to start. Get a key at aistudio.google.com/apikey, then paste it in via ⚙ Settings. Everything works without it - Curated just falls back to plainer matching.

In ⚙ Settings, check off what you actually have access to - paid or free. Green means no extra cost with what you already have, blue means you'd pay extra even with an account you own (like renting on Amazon), and tan means you don't have that one. Nothing is assumed - only what you check shows as yours.
